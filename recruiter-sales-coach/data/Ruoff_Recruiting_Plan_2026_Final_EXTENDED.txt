Vision STATEMENT
Our vision is simple but ambitious: We want to be the dominant mortgage company in every market we enter and become the clear #1 in purchase market share across the Midwest.
We’ll achieve this by giving elite loan officers the autonomy they want, the technology and support they need, and a true family culture that actually backs them. At Ruoff, you don’t have to choose between high production and a great life — you get both. We’re building something special, and we’re looking for high-performing professionals who want to be part of it.
Charisma- KEYS TO SUCCESS
Consistency: Momentum is built through repetition and reliability.
Human First: Every Interaction starts with real human care, empathy, and connection- before any pitch or process.
Authenticity: Be genuine!
Relationships First: Every touchpoint leads with value, not a pitch.
Input: Every meeting, prospect, and conversation gets input into Shape so nothing drops.
Speed: Timely follow-up after events or active conversations is non-negotiable.
Market Awareness: Intel gathered in Area Manager meetings directly shapes your timing and messaging.
AI as an Enhancer: They refine, analyze, and coach. Relationships remain the core.
The Long Game: While we are always working to convert prospects as quickly as possible, recruiting is often a long game. Many times, our most important work is building brand recognition and cultivating genuine personal relationships with prospects while staying consistently present and adding value until the right window of opportunity arises. Not every conversation leads to an immediate move, but every meaningful touchpoint brings us closer to that moment.

PILLAR 3: SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY
A consistent, authentic social media presence is one of the most effective and sustainable recruiting tools we have. Our strategy is focused on building a personal brand and sharing highlights of the Ruoff Mortgage culture that naturally attracts loan officers who value culture, relationships, and professional growth, while also giving them a real look at who we are as people. The minimum expectation is to post 3-4 times each week collectively across both platforms. Minimum of 15-20 new social connections per week per recruiter (actively connecting with loan officer prospects).

The 80/20 Content Approach
Mix
Content Type
Examples & Purpose
80%
Personal & Authentic
Content that shares who we are beyond our professional roles by giving real and relatable glimpses into our lives, experiences, and personalities. These posts are not about promoting or recruiting directly. They focus on showing the human side of us such as family moments, milestones, everyday life, values, and what matters most outside of work.
The goal is to build connection and trust through consistency and authenticity, helping loan officers and others get to know us as people first rather than just professionals.
20%
Ruoff-Focused
Company culture, career opportunities, product highlights, awards, and recruiting campaigns. Community involvement, team spotlights, behind-the-scenes, personal milestones, local market insights, and survey content from 'What's It's Really Like at Ruoff'
Why This Works: Loan Officers considering a career move are not primarily motivated by corporate messaging. They want to see authentic culture, strong leadership, and real people. The 80% personal content builds credibility and trust that makes the 20% Ruoff content far more compelling and believable.

Monthly Meeting Agenda: 5 Core Topics
Topic 1 — Office Needs & Current Openings
Discussion Question: What are the biggest needs within your offices right now?
Please include any current openings and which branches those openings are tied to.
Topic 2 — Active Candidate Conversations
Discussion Question: Are there any active conversations taking place with potential candidates
by you or your Branch Managers?
Topic 3 — Top Recruiting Prospects & Key Relationships
Discussion Question: Who are the top recruiting prospects or relationships you feel are
worth focusing on right now within your market?
Topic 4 — Competitor Activity & Market Intelligence
Discussion Question: Have you noticed any recent competitor activity, Loan Officer movement,
or market changes that could create recruiting opportunities?
Topic 5 — Upcoming Networking & Community Events
Discussion Question: Are there any upcoming networking events, conferences, association meetings, or community activities within the next month that may be beneficial for us to attend?
PILLAR 5: WEEKLY TEAM MEETINGS WITH ADAM
Weekly meetings with Adam provide dedicated time for accountability, skill development, and strategic alignment. These two recurring sessions ensure that recruiting activity stays on track, obstacles are addressed quickly, and the team continuously sharpens its approach.
Weekly Meeting Schedule
Meeting
Purpose & Agenda
Monday Meeting
Weekly review session focused on three core areas: reviewing calls from the prior week, discussing results and what is working or needs adjustment, and refining strategy and priorities for the week ahead. This meeting sets the tone and direction for the entire week’s recruiting activity.
Wednesday Meeting
Mid-week development session used for role playing exercises to sharpen call skills and conversations, or to address any specific needs that arise. This is including building plans and approaches for high-priority prospects, working through challenging situations, or preparing for upcoming conversations. This meeting keeps skills sharp and ensures recruiters feel confident and prepared.

Conversion Rate Expectations
The team will operate with the following baseline conversion assumptions, with a goal of improving these rates over time through coaching and role-playing:
Outreach Attempts → Quality Conversations: 8–10%
Quality Conversations → Executive Calls Scheduled: 20%
Executive Calls Scheduled → Completed: 75%
Executive Calls Completed → Hire: 70–75%
Regular role-playing and call reviews during Wednesday meetings will focus on improving conversion from outreach to quality conversations.
