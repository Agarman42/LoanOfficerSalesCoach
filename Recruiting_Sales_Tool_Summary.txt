Recruiting Sales Playbook
Core Philosophy, Techniques & Scripts
Purpose: This document provides a clear framework for consistent, high-quality recruiting conversations. It focuses on building genuine relationships, asking strong questions, handling objections professionally, and moving qualified candidates toward conversations with senior leadership.
Core Philosophy
Lead with curiosity and respect rather than pressure.
Make every interaction feel personal and targeted.
Focus on long-term relationship building, not just immediate results.
Quality conversations matter more than raw activity volume.
Help candidates see the value of the platform and long-term support, not just the upfront offer.
Senior leadership conversations should be positioned as high-value and low-risk.
Key Sales Techniques
Neutral Openers (Use First)
Start with a short, human opener before the main pitch:
“How’s your week going so far?”
“Working toward Friday?”
“How’s summer treating you?”
“How are things going in your world these days?”
These help drop the guard and make the call feel less like a cold pitch.
Primary Recruiting Opener
“Our executive team has been impressed with the business you’ve been building, and they specifically asked me to reach out to see if you would be open to a short conversation.”
This opener makes the call feel selective rather than generic.
Discovery & Power Questions
Use these to understand what matters most to the candidate:
“What’s your favorite thing about where you’re at right now?”
“What’s keeping you there?”
“What do you love most about your current situation?”
“What’s been working really well for you lately?”
“What would have to be true for you to even consider exploring something new?”
These questions help uncover real motivations and create stronger conversations.
Handling Objections & Pushing for Senior Leadership Conversations
When candidates push back (especially with “I’m happy where I’m at” or “I’m not interested right now”), the goal is to respectfully but confidently push toward a conversation with senior leadership rather than accepting a quick “no.”
Core Mindset When Handling Objections
Acknowledge their position first.
Reframe the value around long-term fit and platform support.
Make it clear that the real decision should be about more than just the sign-on bonus.
Position the senior leadership conversation as low-risk and high-value.
Strong Objection Handling Language
When they say they’re happy or not looking:
“I completely understand. At the same time, with the kind of production you’re putting up, I’d argue you owe it to yourself to put as much weight on the platform and long-term support as you do on the sign-on bonus. When someone’s operating at a high level, the upfront number becomes less of a deciding factor than whether the infrastructure can actually help sustain and grow that success.”
“I respect that. A lot of strong producers we talk to feel the same way initially. What we’ve found is that taking a short conversation with senior leadership often gives people real clarity — even if they ultimately decide to stay where they are.”
“Fair enough. With everything you have going on, it makes sense to be selective. That’s exactly why I think a quick conversation with our leadership team would be worthwhile. They can speak directly to what support and structure look like here, so you can make the best decision for your situation.”
When they mention they just started somewhere new or are under contract:
“I completely get that. A lot of people in that situation still find value in understanding what else is out there, especially when they’re early in a new role. Would you be open to a short conversation with our leadership team just to learn what we have to offer?”
“Totally fair. Since you’re still getting settled, I won’t push. That said, I’d love to keep the door open. Would you be okay if I reached back out in a few months to see how things are going?”
When they hesitate on speaking with senior leadership:
“I understand it can feel like a big step. At the same time, with the production level you’re at, our overall compensation and support structure would be well north of a standard sign-on offer. The real question is whether the platform can actually help you hit your bigger goals long-term. A short conversation with leadership is the best way to get clarity on that.”
“No pressure at all. Many people we speak with aren’t actively looking but still find it valuable to understand what’s possible elsewhere. Our leadership team is very good at having honest, no-pressure conversations about exactly that.”
Ending Calls & Long-Term Nurturing
Even when a candidate is not ready to move forward, always aim to keep the relationship alive:
Ask for a social media connection (LinkedIn or Facebook).
Offer a low-pressure future touchpoint (e.g., “in 3 months,” “in the fall,” or “in 6 months”).
Position future contact as relationship-building rather than constant recruiting pressure.
Example language: - “Would you be open to connecting on social so we can stay in touch?” - “Would it be okay if I checked in with you again in a few months?”
Activity Targets & Metrics
Primary Focus: Move qualified candidates to conversations with senior leadership.
Key Weekly Targets (Per Recruiter): - Outreach Attempts: 270 per week - Quality Conversations: 24–25 per week - Executive / Senior Leadership Calls Scheduled: 4.8–5.1 per week - Executive / Senior Leadership Calls Completed: 3.6–3.8 per week
Conversion Benchmarks: - Outreach → Quality Conversations: 8–10% - Quality Conversations → Senior Leadership Calls Scheduled: 20% - Senior Leadership Calls Scheduled → Completed: 75% - Senior Leadership Calls Completed → Hire: 70–75%
Best Outreach Days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
Common Coaching Points
Pronunciation: Always say the company name cleanly and confidently.
Neutral Openers: Use a short warm-up before the main pitch on most calls.
Listening vs. Talking: Let the candidate speak more than you do, especially when they share personal or career stories.
One Strong Follow-Up Question: On “I’m happy” or hesitant calls, ask at least one good discovery question before pivoting to a future touch.
Focus on Platform Over Bonus: At higher production levels, emphasize long-term support and infrastructure more than the upfront offer.
